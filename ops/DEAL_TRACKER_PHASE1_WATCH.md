# Deal Tracker — Phase 1 (Shadow) Watch Guide

Phase 1 = the tracker READS and SHOWS, moves nothing. You grade its homework.
You manually flip Phase 2 only when it has earned it. It NEVER promotes itself.

## What you'll see on each active card
- Its guessed stage, who it thinks owes the next move, agreement yes/no,
  the EXACT sentence it based that on, and a confidence level (high/med/low).
- The fuzzy ones land in the "Needs your read" lane. Nothing moves.

## GREEN — a good read
- The evidence sentence actually backs the call (you can see WHY it decided).
- "Who owes the move" matches reality (you replied last -> awaiting them).
- Catches a clear yes ("send the invoice") -> flags Ready-to-invoice, does NOT invoice.
- Flags genuinely fuzzy threads to "Needs your read" instead of guessing.
- Low-confidence calls are the actually-murky ones; high-confidence are the obvious ones.

## RED — keep Phase 2 OFF if you see
- Confidently wrong: high confidence on a call that's plainly off. The only dangerous failure.
- Evidence doesn't match the call (says "rates sent" but quotes "we have no budget").
- A decline read as a deal, or a clear agreement missed.
- "Who owes the move" backwards (says you owe a reply when they've gone silent).
- Over-flagging (everything "unsure" = useless) or under-flagging (nothing "unsure" = overconfident).

## The bar to flip Phase 2 (let it move cards)
Over a few days / ~15-20 deals, you can say:
1. On high-confidence reads, you'd have made the same move almost every time.
2. When it's wrong, it failed SAFE (flagged "unsure"), it didn't bluff.
3. The evidence sentence is right basically every time.
4. The "Needs your read" pile is a sensible handful, not 2 or 50.

## The one-line test
"Would I have made the same move it's suggesting?" Track yes/no as you skim.
Near-constant yes on the confident ones + all misses are "it asked, not bluffed" = earned the keys.

## The instinct to protect
A confident WRONG read keeps Phase 2 off. A humble "I'm not sure" never does —
that's the system working as designed. You give it the keys, deliberately. It never takes them.
